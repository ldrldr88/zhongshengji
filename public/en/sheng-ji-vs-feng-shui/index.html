Core Definitions

Sheng Ji: Dragon Vein Energy for a Living Person's Own Destiny

Sheng Ji (生基) places a living person's life information — hair, nail clippings, blood, garment — in a genuine dragon vein plot while the person is still alive. The dragon vein's earth qi bonds with the living client and continuously elevates their destiny in real time. The client themselves is the beneficiary, and effects begin manifesting within months of the ceremony.

Yin Zhai Feng Shui: Dragon Vein Energy Transmitted to Descendants via Ancestors

Yin Zhai Feng Shui (陰宅風水) involves selecting a propitious burial site for a deceased ancestor. The earth qi of the burial location transmits its energy to the ancestor's descendants through the physical remains, benefiting their career, wealth, marriage and fertility across generations. The beneficiaries are the descendants, not the deceased person.

7 Key Differences

Difference 1: Who Benefits

Sheng Ji: the living client benefits directly and personally. Yin Zhai: the deceased person's descendants benefit — the interred individual is already beyond receiving benefit themselves.

Difference 2: How Quickly Effects Appear

Sheng Ji: effects typically begin within 1 to 6 months of the ceremony — facial complexion improvement first, then improved interpersonal relationships, then concrete career and financial changes. Yin Zhai: effects typically require years to decades to manifest, and may only become apparent in the next generation or later.

Difference 3: What Is Placed in the Earth

Sheng Ji: four living items — hair, nail clippings, blood-infused paper, and a worn garment — along with a paper effigy, are interred. Yin Zhai: the physical remains (bones or ashes) of the deceased are interred; the earth qi transmits through the ancestral DNA to living descendants.

Difference 4: Ritual Requirements

Sheng Ji requires a fully ordained Taoist master (with documented lineage) to perform a complete 2-day ceremony including prayers, disaster-dispelling, wealth rituals and empowerment rites. Yin Zhai Feng Shui primarily requires an expert Feng Shui geomancer (地師) skilled in site selection and auspicious date calculation — the ritual elements are comparatively simpler.

Differences 5–7: Dragon Vein Type, Impact on Descendants, Duration

Dragon vein type: Sheng Ji requires a Yang-natured plot with active, upwelling qi; Yin Zhai requires a stable Yin-natured plot with deep, settled qi — not all plots are suitable for both purposes. Impact on descendants: Sheng Ji's benefits are exclusively personal to the client and do not affect descendants positively or negatively. Yin Zhai is specifically designed to benefit descendants. Duration: Sheng Ji has defined time limits (5-year or Lifetime package). Yin Zhai effects theoretically persist across multiple generations as long as the burial site remains undisturbed.

Which Should You Choose?

Choose Sheng Ji When You Need Personal Destiny Improvement Now

If your goal is to improve your own career, wealth, health or relationships — and you want to see meaningful changes within your own lifetime — Sheng Ji is the right choice. It is the most direct and fastest-acting method for personal destiny elevation within the Taoist Feng Shui tradition.

Can You Do Both Sheng Ji and Yin Zhai Feng Shui?

Yes — the two practices operate on different levels and do not interfere with each other. A family seeking comprehensive destiny management might pursue Sheng Ji for the living members who need personal improvement, while also ensuring that recently deceased ancestors are given propitious Yin Zhai burial sites to benefit future generations.
