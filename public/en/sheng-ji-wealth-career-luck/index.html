Why the Dragon Vein Determines Which Benefits Are Strongest

Not All Dragon Vein Plots Are Equal for All Benefit Directions

The dragon vein plot used for Sheng Ji directly determines which destiny directions receive the strongest energetic support. The Fu Gui Plot's Two Dominant Formations

Formation 1 — Official Rank Energy (官貴之氣): the San Tai Xing (Three Terrace Star) mountain formation, confirmed by classical Feng Shui texts as producing official rank and scholarly achievement. Formation 2 — Speed-Wealth Configuration (速發財富): three rivers flowing counter-currently toward the plot (Danshui, Dahan and Xindian Rivers), described in classical texts as the 'black-cap minister' formation — meaning rapid wealth generation at the highest level.

Wealth Activation: The Counter-Current River Advantage

Classical Texts Confirm: Counter-Current Water = Fast Wealth

The three rivers flowing toward the Fu Gui plot create the formation classical texts describe as 'Ni Shui Chao Tang' — counter-current toward the hall. Two classical passages specifically address this formation: 'Counter-current water to the hall, become a black-cap minister' and 'Gather all source waters, descendants buy all land in the world'. Both indicate rapid, large-scale wealth generation as the outcome.

What Wealth Activation Looks Like in Practice

Clients seeking financial improvement consistently report that wealth arrives through multiple unexpected channels rather than a single large event: overdue payments collected, previously stalled deals completed, new income streams appearing, investment decisions that consistently outperform prior patterns. The cumulative effect, rather than a single windfall, is the typical profile.
