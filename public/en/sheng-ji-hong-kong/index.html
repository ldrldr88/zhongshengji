Three hard facts about doing it locally

Hong Kong has no purchasable true dragon-vein land

Authentic Sheng Ji requires burying your materials deep in owned dragon-vein earth. Hong Kong's hills are country park or government land — legally acquiring a qualifying plot is impossible. This is not a skill gap; it is a land reality.

Columbarium and niche 'sheng ji' cannot draw earth qi

Much of what is sold locally places materials in columbarium niches or 'fortune positions.' Plainly: planting means burial in earth; objects on a shelf are severed from the ground and cannot resonate with any vein, however elaborate the accompanying rites.

Covert planting on wild hills backfires

Materials secretly buried on land you do not own risk being dug up and discarded during works — after which they absorb refuse qi that transmits back to you. With something carrying your life information, ownership of the land is a non-negotiable safety line.
