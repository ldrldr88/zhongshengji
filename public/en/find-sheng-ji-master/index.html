The 5 Non-Negotiable Verification Checks

Check 1: Request the Master's Taoist Ordination Details — Specific, Named and Traceable

Ask: who ordained you, when, under what lineage name, and what is your Taoist title? A legitimate answer will be specific and verifiable. Any answer that is vague ('family tradition'), unverifiable or inconsistent across conversations should disqualify the provider.

Check 2: Demand Real Dragon Vein Documentation — Not Just Claims

Ask to see: video footage of the actual dragon vein site (not just promotional photos), land ownership or usage rights documentation, and a specific Feng Shui formation description — which mountain is the origin, what is the mountain star configuration, what is the water configuration. The Fu Gui Shuang Quan plot: origin at Guanyin Mountain, San Tai Xing formation, three-river counter-current water configuration. A provider who cannot deliver formation-level specificity almost certainly does not have a real dragon vein.

Check 3: Request Multiple Full-Length Client Ceremony Videos

Any genuine provider films every ceremony completely and willingly shares this footage with prospective clients before payment is made. Request videos from multiple different clients (not the same ceremony shared repeatedly). Verify the footage is unedited and includes: the opening prayers, the complete Day 1 ritual sequence, the dragon vein site footage, and the core interment step. If a provider refuses under any pretext, walk away.

Check 4: Confirm the Full Price Upfront — No Hidden Fees or Post-Ceremony Additions

A legitimate provider states the complete fee before any money changes hands: what is included, how many payments, and what each payment triggers. Check 5: Verify a Track Record of At Least 10 Years — With Independent Evidence

Search the master's name independently. Look for: media coverage in credible outlets (not just self-published articles), industry association membership records, academic or religious institution affiliations.
