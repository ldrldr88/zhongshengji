Definition: What is Zhong Sheng Ji (Sheng Ji)?

Sheng Ji Defined: Placing a Living Person's Essence in a Dragon Vein

Zhong Sheng Ji (種生基), also known as 'Sheng Ji', 'Living Tomb' or 'Life-Base Feng Shui', is a traditional Taoist geomancy ritual. While the person is still alive, a master selects a true dragon vein plot (Long Xue) and buries the person's hair, nail clippings, blood and a garment in the earth. This creates a permanent energetic bond between the living person and the dragon vein's earth qi (di qi), continuously elevating the person's destiny.

Historical Origins: Over 1,000 Years of Secret Imperial Tradition

Sheng Ji dates back to the Tang and Song dynasties in China, when it was a closely guarded secret practised exclusively by emperors, generals and wealthy merchant families. Only the most accomplished Feng Shui masters were entrusted with selecting Sheng Ji plots. In modern times, the tradition has been preserved within select Taiwanese Feng Shui family lineages, including the Lin family of Zi Xin Ge, who have maintained the practice across three generations.

Sheng Ji vs Traditional Yin Zhai (Ancestral Tomb) Feng Shui

Yin Zhai Feng Shui (陰宅風水) involves selecting burial plots for deceased ancestors to benefit their descendants. Sheng Ji, by contrast, is performed for living persons — the beneficiary experiences the effects themselves, during their own lifetime. Results typically begin manifesting within 1 to 6 months of completion, making it one of the fastest-acting destiny-improvement methods in the Taoist tradition.

The 9 Core Benefits of Sheng Ji

Career & Official Fortune (催官): Promotions, Opportunities, Influential Allies

The dragon vein plot we use — the 'Fu Gui Shuang Quan' plot in Taiwan — carries natural official-rank energy (官貴之氣), as confirmed by classical Feng Shui texts: 'Even if not top of the imperial exams, at least third place.' This makes career advancement and the emergence of helpful, powerful people in one's life one of the most pronounced outcomes of Sheng Ji.

Wealth Activation (招財進祿): Opening Financial Channels

The plot's external water configuration — with the Danshui River, Dahan River and Xindian River all flowing inward toward the plot — creates what classical texts call a 'counter-current wealth formation' (逆水朝堂). Ancient texts state this configuration produces wealth 'as fast as a rising official'. Clients seeking financial breakthroughs consistently report this as the most visibly impactful direction.

Longevity, Protection, Marriage & Fertility

Sheng Ji is also sought for longevity (增壽), warding off misfortune and danger (保命化煞), improving marriage prospects (求婚), and supporting fertility (求子). The ritual's Day 1 ceremony includes a full disaster-dispelling sequence that clears accumulated negative energies from the person's fate chart, laying the foundation for all nine benefit directions.
