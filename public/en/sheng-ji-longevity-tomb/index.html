The Taoist Theory of Lifespan and How Sheng Ji Extends It

Lifespan Is Set by Heaven — But Earth Qi Can Supplement Deficiencies

Taoist cosmology holds that a person's baseline lifespan (shou yuan) is determined by the Heaven Stem and Earth Branch configuration at birth — the BaZi fate chart. However, this baseline is not an immutable ceiling. The earth qi of a genuine dragon vein is the purest life-force energy in nature. By placing the client's life information in the dragon vein, the qi continuously nourishes the client's primordial spirit, supplementing deficiencies in the lifespan element of their fate chart.

Longevity Through Three Dimensions: Body, Mind and Spirit

Sheng Ji longevity effects manifest across three dimensions simultaneously. Physical (身): increased energy, improved sleep quality, enhanced immune function, visible complexion improvement. Mental (心): emotional stability, improved decision clarity, greater psychological resilience under stress. Spiritual (靈): stabilised primordial spirit, increased resistance to external negative energetic influences. All three improve in parallel, creating a compounding effect on overall vitality.

The First Visible Signal: Facial Complexion Changes Within 1 Month

In Chinese physiognomy, three facial zones are directly linked to lifespan and vitality: the forehead (tian ting, associated with heavenly fortune), the space between the brows (yin tang, associated with health), and the tip of the nose (bi zhun, associated with lifespan and wealth). After Sheng Ji, these areas develop a naturally luminous quality that is typically noticed by people close to the client without prompting. This is the earliest observable confirmation that the dragon vein connection is active.
